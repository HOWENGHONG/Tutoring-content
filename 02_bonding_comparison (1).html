Key Substances — Quick Reference
I₂ (Iodine)
Simple Molecular
  • Room temp: solid (but low m.p.)
  • Conducts: No
  • Soluble in water: No ❌
  • Soluble in organic solvents: Yes ✅
  • Low m.p. — weak intermolecular forces
Hg (Mercury)
Metallic
  • Room temp: liquid (only liquid metal)
  • Conducts: Yes ✅ (delocalized e⁻)
  • Not discrete molecules
  • Hg²⁺ ions + delocalized electrons
  • Metallic bonds (weaker than most metals)
Diamond
Giant Covalent
  • M.p.: ~3550°C (very high)
  • Conducts: No ❌ (no free e⁻)
  • Soluble in water/organic solvents: No
  • Hardest natural substance
  • Each C bonded to 4 C atoms
Graphite
Giant Covalent
  • M.p.: high
  • Conducts: Yes ✅ (delocalized e⁻ within layers)
  • Layered structure (weak forces between layers)
  • Each C bonded to 3 C atoms
  • Used as lubricant
Cubic BN
Giant Covalent
  • Diamond-like structure
  • Conducts: No ❌
  • Very hard (used as abrasive)
  • Not brittle (brittleness = ionic property)
  • B and N atoms alternate
OF₂
Simple Molecular
  • NOT giant covalent!
  • O bonded to 2 F atoms (bent shape)
  • Low melting point
  • Does not conduct
  • Common exam trap!
CaO vs NaCl
Ionic
  • CaO: Ca²⁺ + O²⁻ (charges ±2)
  • NaCl: Na⁺ + Cl⁻ (charges ±1)
  • CaO has a higher melting point
  • Reason: higher charges → stronger electrostatic attraction
NaCl Structure
Ionic
  • Each Na⁺ surrounded by 6 Cl⁻
  • Each Cl⁻ surrounded by 6 Na⁺
  • Co-ordination number = 6
  • Giant ionic lattice
How to Identify Bonding Type — Exam Strategy
📋 Decision Steps (given data table)
Step 1 — Check solid-state conductivity
Conducts electricity in the solid state?
✅ Yes → Metallic (delocalized electrons) ❌ No → Go to Step 2
Step 2 — Check melting point
Low melting point (near room temperature)?
✅ Low m.p. → Simple Molecular ❌ High m.p. → Go to Step 3
Step 3 — Check molten / aqueous conductivity
Conducts when molten or dissolved in water?
✅ Yes → Ionic (mobile ions) ❌ No → Giant Covalent
If Giant Covalent — narrow it down further
Conducts → Graphite Does not conduct + hardest → Diamond Does not conduct + hard → SiO₂ / Cubic BN
