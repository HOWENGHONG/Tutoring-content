1.
The coordinates of points A and B are (−3, 5) and (9, −3) respectively. What is the slope of AB?
  • A. −3/2
  • B. −2/3
  • C. 2/3
  • D. 3/2

slope = (−3 − 5) / (9 − (−3)) = −8 / 12 = −2/3
Answer: B
2.
The straight line L: 3x − 5y + 30 = 0 cuts the y-axis at point Q. What are the coordinates of Q?
  • A. (0, −6)
  • B. (0, 6)
  • C. (−10, 0)
  • D. (10, 0)
2 marks
At y-axis, x = 0: −5y + 30 = 0 → y = 6
Answer: B   Q = (0, 6)
3.
P(2, −1) and Q(8, 5) are two points. R divides PQ internally in the ratio 1 : 2. What are the coordinates of R?
  • A. (4, 1)
  • B. (5, 2)
  • C. (6, 3)
  • D. (4, 3)
2 marks
m : n = 1 : 2 → m pairs with Q, n pairs with P
x = (1×8 + 2×2) / 3 = 12/3 = 4
y = (1×5 + 2×(−1)) / 3 = 3/3 = 1
Answer: A   R = (4, 1)
4.
Which of the following pairs of straight lines are perpendicular to each other?
I.  2x − 3y + 1 = 0  and  3x − 2y + 5 = 0
II.  x + 4y − 7 = 0  and  4x − y + 3 = 0
III.  5x − y + 2 = 0  and  x + 5y − 4 = 0
  • A. I only
  • B. II only
  • C. II and III only
  • D. I, II and III
2 marks
I: slopes 2/3 and 3/2 → product = 1 ≠ −1 ✗
II: slopes −1/4 and 4 → product = −1 ✓
III: slopes 5 and −1/5 → product = −1 ✓
Answer: C (II and III only)
5.
The straight lines L₁: kx − 4y + 8 = 0 and L₂: 3x + 6y − 5 = 0 are parallel. Find k.
  • A. k = −2
  • B. k = 2
  • C. k = −½
  • D. k = ½
2 marks
Slope of L₁ = k/4  |  Slope of L₂ = −3/6 = −1/2
Parallel → k/4 = −1/2 → k = −2
Answer: A   k = −2
Section BTrigonometry Applications (Ch.8)3 questions · 12 marks
6.
A road rises 4 m for every 25 m of horizontal distance.
(a)Find the angle of inclination θ of the road, correct to 3 significant figures.
(b)A cyclist travels 200 m along the road (i.e. along the slope). Find the actual vertical height gained. Give your answer correct to 3 significant figures.
4 marks
Working space
(a) tan θ = rise / run = 4 / 25 = 0.16
θ = arctan(0.16) = 9.09° (3 s.f.)
(b) Height gained = 200 × sin 9.09° = 200 × 0.1579 = 31.6 m (3 s.f.)
Note: slope ≠ horizontal distance. The 200 m is along the hypotenuse, so use sin.
7.
From the top of a lighthouse 120 m above sea level, a coastguard observes a boat at sea. The angle of depression of the boat is 25°. At the same moment, the angle of elevation of the top of a cliff directly behind the lighthouse (from the same observation point) is 18°. The cliff is 80 m horizontally from the lighthouse.
(a)Find the horizontal distance between the lighthouse and the boat. Give your answer correct to the nearest metre.
(b)Find the height of the cliff above sea level. Give your answer correct to 3 significant figures.
4 marks
Working space
(a) Draw diagram. Angle of depression = alternate angle inside triangle = 25°
tan 25° = 120 / d → d = 120 / tan 25° = 120 / 0.4663 ≈ 257 m
⚠ Do NOT use the depression angle directly without marking it as the alternate angle in the triangle!
(b) Height of cliff above lighthouse top = 80 × tan 18° = 80 × 0.3249 = 26.0 m
Total height of cliff above sea level = 120 + 26.0 = 146 m (3 s.f.)
8.
A ship leaves port A and sails on a bearing of 130° for 90 km to reach port B. It then changes course and sails on a bearing of 040° for 60 km to reach port C.
(a)Find how far south of A that B is located. (Give your answer correct to 3 s.f.)
(b)Find how far east of A that B is located. (Give your answer correct to 3 s.f.)
(c)Find the total displacement eastward from A to C. (Give your answer correct to 3 s.f.)
4 marks
Working space
(a) Bearing 130° → angle from South = 50° (or angle from North = 130°)
Southward component of AB = 90 cos 50° = 90 × 0.6428 = 57.9 km south
(b) Eastward component of AB = 90 sin 50° = 90 × 0.7660 = 68.9 km east
(c) Bearing 040° → angle from North = 40°
Eastward component of BC = 60 sin 40° = 60 × 0.6428 = 38.6 km
Total eastward from A to C = 68.9 + 38.6 = 107 km east (3 s.f.)
Tip: For any bearing θ from North → North component = distance × cos θ, East component = distance × sin θ

9.
The coordinates of points A and B are (−4, 3) and (8, −1) respectively.
(a)Find the distance AB.
(b)Find the coordinates of the midpoint M of AB.
3 marks
Working space
(a) AB = √[(8−(−4))² + (−1−3)²] = √[144 + 16] = √160 = 4√10
(b) M = ((−4+8)/2, (3+(−1))/2) = (2, 1)
10.
The straight line L: 5x − 2y + 20 = 0 cuts the x-axis at A and the y-axis at B.
(a)Find the coordinates of A and B.
(b)Find the slope of L.
3 marks
Working space
(a) x-axis (y=0): 5x+20=0 → x=−4 → A=(−4, 0)
     y-axis (x=0): −2y+20=0 → y=10 → B=(0, 10)
(b) From 5x−2y+20=0: slope = −5/(−2) = 5/2
Slope from general form ax+by+c=0: slope = −a/b. Here a=5, b=−2 → slope = −5/(−2) = 5/2
11.
Find the equation of the straight line passing through (−2, 7) with slope −3. Give your answer in the general form.
3 marks
Working space
y − 7 = −3(x − (−2)) = −3(x + 2)
y − 7 = −3x − 6 → 3x + y − 1 = 0
3x + y − 1 = 0
⚠ General form must have no fractions. Check: no fractions here ✓
12.
It is given that the straight line L₁: 4x − 3y + 9 = 0. Find the equation of L₂ which is perpendicular to L₁ and passes through the point (8, −2). Give your answer in the general form.
3 marks
Working space
Slope of L₁ = 4/3 → slope of L₂ = −3/4 (perpendicular: m₁ × m₂ = −1)
y − (−2) = −3/4 · (x − 8) → 4(y+2) = −3(x−8)
4y + 8 = −3x + 24 → 3x + 4y − 16 = 0
3x + 4y − 16 = 0
13.
The straight lines L₁: 3x + 4y − 12 = 0 and L₂: x − 2y + 4 = 0 intersect at point A. Find the coordinates of A.
3 marks
Working space
From L₂: x = 2y − 4
Sub into L₁: 3(2y−4) + 4y − 12 = 0 → 10y = 24 → y = 12/5
x = 2(12/5) − 4 = 24/5 − 20/5 = 4/5
A = (4/5, 12/5)
14.
P(4, k) lies on the line segment joining A(−2, 1) and B(10, 7). Find the value of k and the ratio AP : PB.
3 marks
Working space
Let AP : PB = m : n. x-coord of P: (m×10 + n×(−2)) / (m+n) = 4
10m − 2n = 4m + 4n → 6m = 6n → AP : PB = 1 : 1
So P is the midpoint of AB: k = (1+7)/2 = k = 4
Section DMedium Questions5 questions · 27 marks
15.
It is given that straight lines L₁: hx + 3y − 15 = 0 and L₂: (h + 6)x + 12y + 11 = 0 are parallel.
(a)Find h.
(b)Find the y-intercepts of L₁ and L₂ respectively. Hence find the distance between the two y-intercepts.
5 marks
Working space
(a) Slope of L₁ = −h/3  |  Slope of L₂ = −(h+6)/12
Parallel: −h/3 = −(h+6)/12 → 12h = 3(h+6) → 12h = 3h+18 → 9h = 18 → h = 2
(b) L₁: 2x+3y−15=0 → x=0: 3y=15 → y=5 → y-int = (0, 5)
     L₂: 8x+12y+11=0 → x=0: 12y=−11 → y=−11/12 → y-int = (0, −11/12)
Distance = 5 − (−11/12) = 5 + 11/12 = 71/12 units
16.
It is given that the straight line L₁: 2x − 5y + 10 = 0 and the straight line L₂ are perpendicular to each other and intersect at a point on the y-axis.
(a)Find the equation of L₂ in the general form.
(b)Find the area of the triangle bounded by L₁, L₂ and the x-axis.
5 marks
Working space
(a) L₁ meets y-axis (x=0): −5y+10=0 → y=2 → intersection = (0, 2)
Slope of L₁ = 2/5 → slope of L₂ = −5/2 (perpendicular)
L₂ through (0, 2): y−2 = −5/2 · x → 2y−4 = −5x → 5x + 2y − 4 = 0
(b) L₁ x-int (y=0): 2x+10=0 → x=−5 → A=(−5, 0)
     L₂ x-int (y=0): 5x−4=0 → x=4/5 → B=(4/5, 0)
Base = |4/5 − (−5)| = 29/5  |  Height = 2 (y-coord of vertex)
Area = ½ × 29/5 × 2 = 29/5 = 5.8 sq. units
17.
The coordinates of P are (5, −7). P is reflected along the y-axis to Q. Q is then rotated anticlockwise about the origin O through 90° to R. The straight line L passes through Q and R.
(a)Find the coordinates of Q and R.
(b)Find the equation of L in the general form.
(c)Find the y-intercept of L.
6 marks
Working space
(a) Reflect P(5,−7) along y-axis: x → −x → Q = (−5, −7)
Rotate Q 90° anticlockwise: (x, y) → (−y, x) → (7, −5) → R = (7, −5)
(b) Slope of QR = (−5−(−7)) / (7−(−5)) = 2/12 = 1/6
Through Q(−5,−7): y+7 = (1/6)(x+5) → 6y+42 = x+5 → x − 6y − 37 = 0
(c) x=0: −6y−37=0 → y = −37/6 → y-intercept = −37/6
Rotation rule: 90° anticlockwise → (x, y) maps to (−y, x)
18.
The coordinates of points A, B and C are (−2, 0), (4, 12) and (3, 5) respectively. M is the midpoint of AB.
(a)Find the slope of AB and the coordinates of M.
(b)Show that CM ⊥ AB.
(c)Hence prove that △ABC is an isosceles triangle with CA = CB. Also find the area of △ABC.
6 marks
Working space
(a) Slope of AB = (12−0)/(4−(−2)) = 12/6 = 2
M = ((−2+4)/2, (0+12)/2) = (1, 6)
(b) Slope of CM = (5−6)/(3−1) = −1/2
Slope AB × slope CM = 2 × (−1/2) = −1 ✓
∴ CM ⊥ AB  (Q.E.D.)
(c) Since CM is the perpendicular bisector of AB (CM⊥AB and M is midpoint of AB),
every point on CM is equidistant from A and B.
Since C lies on CM → CA = CB → △ABC is isosceles.  (Q.E.D.)
CA² = (3−(−2))² + (5−0)² = 25+25 = 50 → CA = 5√2
CB² = (3−4)² + (5−12)² = 1+49 = 50 → CB = 5√2 ✓
AB = √[(4−(−2))²+(12−0)²] = √[36+144] = √180 = 6√5
CM = √[(3−1)²+(5−6)²] = √[4+1] = √5
Area △ABC = ½ × AB × CM = ½ × 6√5 × √5 = ½ × 6 × 5 = 15 sq. units
19.
It is given that the straight lines L₁: kx − 3y + 15 = 0 and L₂: 5x + (2−k)y + 30 = 0 are parallel to each other.
(a)Find k.
(b)Find the area of the parallelogram bounded by L₁, L₂, the straight line y = −5 and the x-axis.
5 marks
Working space
(a) Slope of L₁ = k/3  |  Slope of L₂ = −5/(2−k)
Parallel: k/3 = −5/(2−k) → k(2−k) = −15 → 2k−k² = −15 → k²−2k−15 = 0
(k−5)(k+3) = 0 → k = 5 or k = −3
If k=5: slope L₁ = 5/3, slope L₂ = −5/(−3) = 5/3 ✓ | L₂: 5x−3y+30=0 (parallel ✓)
If k=−3: slope L₁ = −1, slope L₂ = −5/5 = −1 ✓ | both work
k = 5 or k = −3
(b) Take k=5: L₁: 5x−3y+15=0, L₂: 5x−3y+30=0
Slope = 5/3. The region is between y=0 (x-axis) and y=−5.
Height of parallelogram = |−5 − 0| = 5
L₁ meets x-axis: 5x+15=0 → x=−3 → (−3,0)
L₂ meets x-axis: 5x+30=0 → x=−6 → (−6,0)
Base (along x-axis between the two lines) = |−3−(−6)| = 3
Area = base × height = 3 × 5 = 15 sq. units
Note: For k=−3 the answer differs; check which value your teacher prefers.

20.
In a rectangular coordinate plane, the coordinates of points A, B and C are (2, 10), (6, −2) and (14, 4) respectively. Let P be a point on AC such that BP is an altitude of △ABC.
(a)Find the equation of the straight line which passes through B and P.
(b)Find the coordinates of P.
(c)Find the ratio of the area of △ABC to the area of △BCP.
6 marks
Working space
(a) Slope of AC = (4−10)/(14−2) = −6/12 = −1/2
BP ⊥ AC → slope of BP = 2
Line BP through B(6,−2): y+2 = 2(x−6) → 2x−y−14 = 0
Equation of BP: 2x − y − 14 = 0
(b) Line AC through A(2,10), slope −1/2: y−10 = −1/2(x−2) → x+2y = 22
Solve simultaneously: 2x−y=14 and x+2y=22
From first: y=2x−14. Sub: x+2(2x−14)=22 → 5x=50 → x=10, y=6
P = (10, 6)
(c) △ABC and △BCP share the same height from B perpendicular to AC.
Area ratio = AC : PC (same height h from B)
AP² = (10−2)²+(6−10)² = 64+16 = 80 → AP = 4√5
PC² = (14−10)²+(4−6)² = 16+4 = 20 → PC = 2√5
AC = AP + PC = 4√5 + 2√5 = 6√5
Area △ABC : Area △BCP = AC : PC = 6√5 : 2√5 = 3 : 1
Verify: Area △ABC = ½|det| = ½|(6−2)(4−10)−(14−2)(−2−10)| = ½|−24+144| = 60; Area △BCP = ½×20 = 20. Ratio = 3:1 ✓
21.
In a rectangular coordinate plane, P(30, 30) and Q(36, 0) are two points. Denote O as the origin.
(a)Find the equation of the perpendicular bisector of PQ.
(b)Find the coordinates of the circumcentre of △OPQ.
5 marks
Working space
(a) Midpoint of PQ = ((30+36)/2, (30+0)/2) = (33, 15)
Slope of PQ = (0−30)/(36−30) = −5
Slope of perp. bisector = 1/5
Through (33,15): y−15 = 1/5(x−33) → 5y−75 = x−33 → x − 5y + 42 = 0
(b) The circumcentre lies on all three perpendicular bisectors. Find perp. bisector of OP:
O=(0,0), P=(30,30). Midpoint=(15,15). Slope OP=1 → perp slope=−1
Perp bisector of OP: y−15=−(x−15) → x+y=30   ... (ii)
Solve (i) x−5y=−42 and (ii) x+y=30:
(ii)−(i): 6y=72 → y=12. x=30−12=18
Circumcentre = (18, 12)
Check: distance to O=√(324+144)=√468; to P=√(144+324)=√468; to Q=√(324+144)=√468 ✓
22.
In a rectangular coordinate plane, A(−39, 0), B(0, −52) and C(26, 0) are the vertices of △ABC. Denote the orthocentre of △ABC by H.
(a) (i)Find the equation of the altitude from vertex A to base BC.
   (ii)Hence find the coordinates of H.
(b)Someone claims that A, H and the centroid G of △ABC are collinear. Do you agree? Explain your answer.
5 marks
Working space
(a)(i) Slope of BC = (0−(−52))/(26−0) = 52/26 = 2
Altitude from A ⊥ BC → slope = −1/2
Through A(−39,0): y = −1/2(x+39) → x+2y+39=0 → x + 2y + 39 = 0
(ii) Altitude from C to AB: slope AB=(−52−0)/(0−(−39))=−4/3 → slope=3/4
Through C(26,0): y=(3/4)(x−26) → 3x−4y−78=0
Solve x+2y=−39 and 3x−4y=78:
×2: 2x+4y=−78; add: 5x=0 → x=0, y=−39/2
H = (0, −39/2)
(b) Centroid G = ((−39+0+26)/3, (0+(−52)+0)/3) = (−13/3, −52/3)
Slope of AH = (−39/2−0)/(0−(−39)) = (−39/2)/39 = −1/2
Slope of AG = (−52/3−0)/(−13/3−(−39)) = (−52/3)/((−13+117)/3) = (−52/3)/(104/3) = −1/2
Both slopes = −1/2 and both pass through A → A, H, G collinear.
Agree ✓ — A, H, and G all lie on the same line (the Euler line of △ABC).
23.
△ABC is bounded by the straight lines L₁: x + y − 10 = 0, L₂: x − 7y + 38 = 0 and L₃: 3x − y + 14 = 0.
(a)Find the vertices A, B, C of △ABC (A = L₁∩L₂, B = L₁∩L₃, C = L₂∩L₃).
(b)Show that △ABC is an isosceles triangle by computing the lengths of its sides.
2 marks + 2 marks
Working space
(a) A = L₁∩L₂: x+y=10 and x−7y=−38
Subtract: 8y=48 → y=6, x=4 → A=(4,6)
B = L₁∩L₃: x+y=10 and 3x−y=−14
Add: 4x=−4 → x=−1, y=11 → B=(−1,11)
C = L₂∩L₃: x−7y=−38 and 3x−y=−14
×3: 3x−21y=−114; subtract: −20y=−100 → y=5, x=−3 → C=(−3,5)
(b) AB²=(−1−4)²+(11−6)²=25+25=50 → AB=5√2
BC²=(−3−(−1))²+(5−11)²=4+36=40 → BC=2√10
AC²=(−3−4)²+(5−6)²=49+1=50 → AC=5√2
AB = AC = 5√2 → △ABC is isosceles ✓
