Q1
Ch.1 Speed
1 mark
A mouse runs 600 m in 5 minutes. What is its average speed in m/s?
A. 0.5 m/s
B. 2 m/s
C. 120 m/s
D. 300 m/s
✅ Answer: B. 2 m/s
First convert: 5 min = 5 × 60 = 300 s
speed = 600 ÷ 300 = 2 m/s
⚠️ Common mistake: forgetting to convert minutes → seconds!
Q2
Ch.1 Speed vs Velocity
1 mark
A cat runs 400 m north, then 300 m east. The total time taken is 100 s. Which of the following is correct?
A. Average speed = 7 m/s, average velocity = 7 m/s
B. Average speed = 7 m/s, average velocity = 5 m/s N53°E
C. Average speed = 5 m/s, average velocity = 7 m/s
D. Average speed = 5 m/s, average velocity = 5 m/s
✅ Answer: B
Total distance = 400 + 300 = 700 m  →  speed = 700 ÷ 100 = 7 m/s
Displacement = √(400² + 300²) = √250000 = 500 m (straight line)
velocity = 500 ÷ 100 = 5 m/s, direction N53°E
⚠️ Speed uses total distance. Velocity uses displacement (straight line) + must state direction.
Q3
Ch.1 d-t Graph
1 mark
On a distance-time graph, a straight line with a steep positive gradient means the object is:
A. Stationary
B. Moving slowly at constant speed
C. Moving fast at constant speed
D. Accelerating
✅ Answer: C. Moving fast at constant speed
On a d-t graph: gradient = speed. Steeper slope = faster speed. A straight line (not curved) = constant speed.
Q4
Ch.1 d-t Graph
1 mark
The graph below shows object P and object Q on the same distance-time graph. P is a straight line with gentle slope. Q is a straight line with steeper slope. Which statement is correct?
A. P is faster than Q
B. P and Q have the same speed
C. Q is faster than P
D. Both are stationary
✅ Answer: C. Q is faster than P
Steeper gradient on a d-t graph = greater speed. Q has the steeper slope, so Q is moving faster.

Q6
Ch.10 Wave Types
1 mark
In a transverse wave, how do particles vibrate relative to the direction the wave travels?
A. Parallel to wave direction
B. Perpendicular (at 90°) to wave direction
C. In circles
D. They do not vibrate
✅ Answer: B. Perpendicular to wave direction
Transverse = particles vibrate at 90° to the direction of travel. Examples: light, water waves, all EM waves.
Longitudinal = particles vibrate parallel (along) the direction of travel. Example: sound.

A wave has frequency 200 Hz and wavelength 0.5 m. What is its wave speed?
A. 400 m/s
B. 100 m/s
C. 0.0025 m/s
D. 200.5 m/s
✅ Answer: B. 100 m/s
v = f × λ = 200 × 0.5 = 100 m/s
Q8
Ch.10 Period
1 mark
A wave completes 25 cycles in 5 seconds. What is its period?
A. 125 s
B. 5 s
C. 0.2 s
D. 0.04 s
✅ Answer: C. 0.2 s
First find frequency: f = 25 ÷ 5 = 5 Hz
Then: T = 1 ÷ f = 1 ÷ 5 = 0.2 s

Q13
Ch.11 Wavelength vs Frequency
1 mark
As frequency increases across the EM spectrum, what happens to wavelength?
A. It also increases
B. It decreases
C. It stays the same
D. It depends on the medium
✅ Answer: B. It decreases
Since c = f × λ and c is constant (3×10⁸ m/s), if f goes up, λ must go down. They are inversely proportional.

A. Horizontal distance from one crest to the next
B. Vertical distance from crest to trough
C. Vertical distance from rest position to a crest

✅ Answer: C
Amplitude = distance from the rest/equilibrium position to a crest (or to a trough).
⚠️ NOT crest to trough — that is 2× the amplitude.

Q16
Ch.1 — Speed vs Velocity
7 marks
A dog runs from point P to point R via point Q. PQ = 400 m (heading east), QR = 300 m (heading south). The dog's average speed is 2 m/s.

(a) Find the total distance travelled and the time taken. (2 marks)
(b) Find the dog's displacement (magnitude and direction) from P to R. (2 marks)
(c) Find the dog's average velocity. (2 marks)
(d) A cat starts from P at the same time. The cat travels in a straight line directly from P to R. The cat's average speed is the same as the dog's average speed (2 m/s). Who arrives at R first? Show working. (1 mark)
Working space
(a) Total distance = 400 + 300 = 700 m
time = distance ÷ speed = 700 ÷ 2 = 350 s

(b) Displacement = √(400² + 300²) = √(160000 + 90000) = √250000 = 500 m
Direction: S53°E (or bearing 127°) — from P to R, east then south

(c) Average velocity = displacement ÷ time = 500 ÷ 350 = 1.43 m/s, direction S53°E
⚠️ Must state the direction! A number alone is NOT a velocity.

(d) Cat travels straight line PR = 500 m at 2 m/s
cat's time = 500 ÷ 2 = 250 s
Dog's time = 350 s > 250 s → Cat arrives first (100 s earlier)
Q17
Ch.1 — d-t Graph Reading
5 marks
A distance-time graph shows the journey of a cyclist: (a) Describe the motion in each stage. (3 marks)
(b) Calculate the speed in Stage 1. (1 mark)
(c) Calculate the speed in Stage 3. (1 mark)
Working space
(a)
Stage 1 (0–40 s): Moving at constant speed (straight line, positive gradient)
Stage 2 (40–60 s): Stationary (flat/horizontal line, gradient = 0)
Stage 3 (60–100 s): Moving at constant speed (straight line, positive gradient — different slope to stage 1)

(b) Speed in Stage 1 = gradient = (200 − 0) ÷ (40 − 0) = 200 ÷ 40 = 5 m/s

(c) Speed in Stage 3 = gradient = (400 − 200) ÷ (100 − 60) = 200 ÷ 40 = 5 m/s
(Same speed in both stages in this case!)
📐 Wave Formula Reminder
v = f × λ    (wave speed = frequency × wavelength)
T = 1 ÷ f    (period = 1 ÷ frequency)
Units: v in m/s · f in Hz · λ in m · T in s
Q18
Ch.10 — Wave Calculations
6 marks
A sound wave travels at 340 m/s and has a wavelength of 0.85 m.

(a) Calculate the frequency of the sound wave. (2 marks)
(b) Calculate the period of the wave. (2 marks)
(c) The sound wave enters water, where its speed increases to 1500 m/s. The frequency does NOT change. Calculate the new wavelength in water. (2 marks)
Working space
(a) Rearrange v = fλ: f = v ÷ λ = 340 ÷ 0.85 = 400 Hz

(b) T = 1 ÷ f = 1 ÷ 400 = 0.0025 s (= 2.5 × 10⁻³ s)

(c) Rearrange v = fλ: λ = v ÷ f = 1500 ÷ 400 = 3.75 m
Notice: when speed increases and frequency stays the same, wavelength increases too.
📡 EM Spectrum Reminder
Low → High frequency: Radio · Microwave · Infrared · Visible · UV · X-ray · Gamma
All EM waves travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s in vacuum and are transverse waves.

Q20
Ch.10 — Compare Wave Types
4 marks
(a) State TWO differences between transverse waves and longitudinal waves. Give one example of each. (4 marks)
Working space
Any two differences (1 mark each), plus examples (1 mark each):

Difference 1: In transverse waves, particles vibrate perpendicular (90°) to the direction of wave travel. In longitudinal waves, particles vibrate parallel (along) the direction of travel.

Difference 2: Transverse waves have crests and troughs. Longitudinal waves have compressions and rarefactions.

Examples: Transverse = light / water waves / all EM waves. Longitudinal = sound.
