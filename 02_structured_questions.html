Part 4

Mole Calculations — Gas Volume

Practice the exact question types from the exam syllabus.

n = m / M
moles = mass ÷ molar mass
n = V / 24
gas moles = volume ÷ 24 dm³ (RTP)
c = n / V
concentration = moles ÷ volume (dm³)
Question 1 — from Exam Syllabus (Worksheet p.2)
Calculate the volume of carbon dioxide produced at room temperature and pressure when an excess of dilute hydrochloric acid is added to 1.000 g of calcium carbonate.

CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + CO₂ + H₂O

(Relative atomic masses: Ca = 40, C = 12, O = 16)
  1. Find molar mass of CaCO₃:
    M(CaCO₃) = 40 + 12 + (16×3) = 100 g/mol
  2. Find moles of CaCO₃:
    n(CaCO₃) = m ÷ M = 1.000 ÷ 100 = 0.01 mol
  3. Find moles of CO₂ (from equation):
    Mole ratio CaCO₃ : CO₂ = 1 : 1
    ∴ n(CO₂) = 0.01 mol
  4. Find volume of CO₂ at RTP:
    V = n × 24 = 0.01 × 24 = 0.24 dm³
    = 240 cm³
✅ Answer: Volume of CO₂ = 0.24 dm³ (240 cm³)
Question 2 — from Exam Syllabus (Worksheet p.2)
Aluminium reacts with dilute hydrochloric acid.

Balance the equation: Al + HCl → AlCl₃ + H₂

Then calculate the volume of hydrogen gas produced at RTP when 2.70 g of aluminium reacts completely with excess HCl.

(Relative atomic mass: Al = 27)
  1. Balance the equation:
    2Al + 6HCl → 2AlCl₃ + 3H₂
  2. Find moles of Al:
    n(Al) = 2.70 ÷ 27 = 0.1 mol
  3. Find moles of H₂ (from equation):
    Mole ratio Al : H₂ = 2 : 3
    n(H₂) = 0.1 × (3/2) = 0.15 mol
  4. Find volume of H₂ at RTP:
    V = n × 24 = 0.15 × 24 = 3.6 dm³
✅ Answer: Volume of H₂ = 3.6 dm³
Question 3 — Extra Practice
Zinc reacts with dilute sulfuric acid according to:

Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂

Calculate the volume of hydrogen gas produced at RTP when 6.54 g of zinc reacts with excess sulfuric acid.

(Relative atomic mass: Zn = 65.4)
  1. Moles of Zn:
    n(Zn) = 6.54 ÷ 65.4 = 0.1 mol
  2. Moles of H₂:
    Mole ratio Zn : H₂ = 1 : 1
    n(H₂) = 0.1 mol
  3. Volume at RTP:
    V = 0.1 × 24 = 2.4 dm³
✅ Answer: Volume of H₂ = 2.4 dm³
Question 4 — Reverse Calculation
When excess zinc reacts with dilute sulfuric acid, 4.8 dm³ of hydrogen gas is collected at RTP.

Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂

Calculate the mass of zinc that reacted.

(Relative atomic mass: Zn = 65.4)
  1. Moles of H₂:
    n(H₂) = V ÷ 24 = 4.8 ÷ 24 = 0.2 mol
  2. Moles of Zn:
    Mole ratio Zn : H₂ = 1 : 1
    n(Zn) = 0.2 mol
  3. Mass of Zn:
    m = n × M = 0.2 × 65.4 = 13.08 g
✅ Answer: Mass of Zn = 13.08 g
